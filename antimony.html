Characteristics:
  • Chemical symbol is Sb.
  • Chemically fairly stable, but reacts with oxygen at high temperatures.
  • Exists in several allotropes, the stable form at room temperature is rhombic crystalline.
  • Known for its toxicity in large amounts.
  • Used in metallic alloys, flame retardants, pigments, and electronic devices.
Technological Applications:
Used in flame retardants, alloys (lead-acid batteries, bearing metals), semiconductors, and compounds (match industry).
Deposit Locations:
Major deposits in China, Russia, Bolivia, and Kyrgyzstan. In Italy, the most important mine was Su Suergiu in Sardinia.
Health Effects:
Exposure can cause irritation to eyes, skin, and lungs, and potential heart, lung, and gastrointestinal problems.
Environmental Impact:
Can cause soil and water pollution, with toxicity to wildlife and potential health impacts on humans through exposure.
