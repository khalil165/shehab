Characteristics

Yttrium is a silvery-grey, highly crystalline metal belonging to the rare earth group. It is stable in air due to a protective oxide film, but oxidizes quickly when heated. It reacts with water releasing hydrogen and with mineral acids. Chips can ignite above 400 Â°C. In finely divided form, it is unstable in air.

Deposit Locations

Yttrium is not found in its free form, but is contained in rare earth minerals such as gadolinite, xenotime, and euxenite.

Health Effects

Yttrium is found in household devices like color TVs, fluorescent lamps, and special glass. In workplaces, it may cause pulmonary embolisms and increase the risk of lung cancer if inhaled over long periods. It can accumulate in the liver, posing a health risk.

Environmental Effects

It is released into the environment from industries and discarded devices. It accumulates in soil and water, increasing risks to humans and animals. In aquatic organisms, it damages cell membranes and may impair reproduction and the nervous system.
