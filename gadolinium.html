What is Gadolinium?

Gadolinium is a chemical element of the rare earth group, with the symbol Gd and atomic number 64. It is known for its unique magnetic properties and is used in various technological sectors due to its reactivity and ability to absorb neutrons.

Technological Applications

Gadolinium is used in many modern electronic devices. It is present:

  • In speakers and microphones, thanks to its strong magnets.
  • In smartphone and TV screens, where it helps improve color and brightness quality.
  • In miniaturized magnetic sensors, such as those used in cell phones and medical devices.
  • In the medical field, in contrast agents for magnetic resonance imaging (MRI).

Where is it found in cell phones?

In cell phones, gadolinium is found in very small quantities, mainly within the magnets of vibration motors, in microphones, and in audio components. It can also be present in integrated circuits in the form of small alloys to improve their efficiency.

Location of Deposits

Gadolinium is not found pure in nature but is extracted from minerals such as monazite and bastnasite. The main world producers are China, the United States, India, and Brazil.

Environmental Effects

  • It can contaminate soil and water if not disposed of correctly.
  • It is toxic to plants and animals at high concentrations.
  • Recycling devices containing it is difficult but important.

Health Effects

Elemental gadolinium is not very dangerous, but some of its compounds can be toxic, especially if inhaled or ingested in large quantities. In medicine, it is used safely in controlled forms.
