TERBIUM

Tb

It has been discovered in 1843 by the swedish chemist Carl Gustaf Mosander,

there isn't much of it: about 1.2 ppm (parts per million).

As you see in the picture on the right, the element is silvered plus scientists discovered that it's soft,

so easy to work with. It's pretty stable in air, although it may oxidize slowly,

creating a protective oxide layer. It has unique magnetic properties and that's why it is used in

advanced magnetic materials. It is also used in a few components of your smartphone, like: screens,

vibration motor and eletronic circuits.

You can find it in China, USA, Australia, Sweden and africa.

It's toxic if ingested and irritating to skin and eyes in case of direct contact.

Even if the mining and processing of terbium-containing minerals have a bad impact on the enviroment,

the element isn't harmful to it under normal contitions.
